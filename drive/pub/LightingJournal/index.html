Henry Ledbetter

Lighting Journal


SXMPRA and Teddy Slugz

This is a video from a concert I went to with my roommate featuring Sxmpra and Teddy Slugz, I really liked the lighting in this video because it has all blue top/back light and all magenta/purple front/up light which compliment each other really well.

Omnia Trip 1

This is from the Omnia Night Club when I was in Vegas for spring break, it's probably the coolest place I've ever been (I know we don't like the c word but its f***in cool). Their chanelier comes down right on you on the dance floor and it feels like its going to hit you right as the beat drops.

Omnia Trip 2

Speaking of Omnia and their dope chandelier- you can also look at it from the balcony above so when I was back in Vegas over the summer to work a gig I had to go back and see it from above.

Robin Thick On Freemont Street

This is also from my summer trip to Vegas, this was a free concert that we didn't even intend on going to but we just walked down the street and there was Robin Thicke. I don't have much to say about their lighting besides the fact that it's cool the producer still put money into the show even though it was free.

Museam Of Light

When I was driving from St. Louis to Fort Collins over the summer, a drive I have taken many times, I noticed a billboard that said "Museam of Light" so obviously I got off the highway at the exit advertised and went to check it out. It was beyond my wildest expectations (more projections than light but still) this room was completely covered in projectors floor to ceiling and they had some really cool animations running.

Dead and Company at the sphere

From yet another trip to Vegas- I swear I'm not a gambling addict this one was with my parents over a year ago but it's the Grateful Dead at the Sphere so obviously it goes in the lighting journal. I still have no idea how they did this effect with the screens but they made the spherical space feel like a rectangular prism and I lost my mind.

Bones

This is Bones, a rapper, performing at GreyDay in St. Louis- I honestly hate when artists do this- making them turn off the lights and have the audience pull their flashlights out. I think it looked good back when it was done with lighters but now that its ugly white LEDs it just looks tacky.

Bones but good

Now this is what I like- lights reacting with the music and in your face.

Apartment Setup

This is what I have for when my roommates and I throw parties- I just have them on sound activated mode so I can enjoy my own parties but they work decently well and are a very fun addition. I also like whoever made the sound mode's style- "if we don't know what they're playing might as well just go crazy" seems to be their mindset which is so chaotic but I love it.

Tulsa King Clip

This is just a clip from the show Tulsa King- I noticed what was suposed to look like a window but very clearly was created by a gobo of sorts so I kept it as somthing to avoid doing.

Projection Mapped Castle

I saw this on instagram- I just can't believe how well they were able to map the building behind them, and I really like this style of in your face lighting.

Gateway Studios

This is Gateway Studios in St. Louis, it's a rehearsal space for touring shows and I got to tour it because the company my Dad works for was doing the electrical contracting. I really enjoyed seeing this space because it really shows the limitless possibilities when you just have a massive open space and a grid.

Powell Symphony Hall

This is Powell Symphony Hall- also in St. Louis, as you might have guessed it's where the Symphony plays and once again I got to tour it because the company my Dad works for was doing electrical work there. It was crazy to see behind (and above) the literal plaster line because this theater is old enough that it's actually just a bunch of plaster suspended 20ish feet down from the ceiling where all the lights are hidden.

Mt. Antero

This is Mount Antero in South Western Colorado- I've climbed this mountain twice and also have a tattoo of it so it obviously is very special to me but mountains in general will forever inspire my lighting with their jagged edges and bright whites.

Casino

I saw this on instagram and I thought it was funny because as a designer who has also worked corperate gigs I am seeing everything he's talking about but am also like ooooo look at the pretty purple on the ceiling.

Dexter in the window.

This is my cat laying down in the window- I just don't think you can ever have enough pictures of cats or sun rays and this has both.

Ditch.

This is the ditch by my friend Kara's house- we like to have little fire pits down there and when we were there over thanksgiving I noticed this shaddow from the neighbors fence that was just impeccable. The cops came later because of course someone called them (they said we weren't doing anything wrong for the record) but I've been kicking myself ever since that I didn't get a picture of this with the red and blue side light.

Focus Pallettes

This was just a video of a guy making focus palletes but I really enjoyed this look where the lights in the back are focused front and center and the ones in front of them are focused straight down.

Galaxy light

This is one of those little astronaut galaxy lights but I really like this effect it uses for the galaxy- its unlike anything we see in real life or that I've seen in performance lighting.

Cable Lights

I saw this on instagram- I loved the industrial look here and how the cables were also lights.

Somebody's Previz

This was from Instagram as well- somebody doing previz for a show but I personally loved this idea of a super long stage with long arrays of lights above even though it's probably very rarely practical to do something like that.

Laser Mirror.

Once again from Instagram- this is a laser being shot at a mirror and if there was ever a way to do this without risking people getting blinded I think it would make the coolest effect ever.
